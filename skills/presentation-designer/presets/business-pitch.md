# Business Pitch Preset

Executive-grade presentation design for investors, C-suite, and high-stakes customer pitches. Inspired by Apple keynote aesthetics — minimal, emotional, visually striking.

## Narrative Architecture

Use the **hero's journey** framework applied to business:

1. **The World As It Is** — establish status quo, the problem everyone feels
2. **The Catalyst** — the insight, data point, or shift that changes everything
3. **The Vision** — what the world could look like (your solution)
4. **The Proof** — evidence it works (traction, case studies, data)
5. **The Path Forward** — how to get there (roadmap, ask, next steps)

### Opening Hook (first 60 seconds)
Start with ONE of:
- A provocative question the audience can't ignore
- A surprising statistic that reframes the problem
- A brief story (15 seconds) that makes the problem personal
- A bold statement that challenges conventional wisdom

### Key Message Hierarchy
- **3 core messages maximum** — if you can't say it in three, you haven't refined enough
- Each message should be a complete sentence, not a topic
- Test: would this message work as a tweet?

### Closing
- Circle back to the opening hook
- End with a clear, single call-to-action
- Last slide should be memorable, not "Thank You"

## Slide Structure (20-30 slides)

### Act 1: The Problem (slides 1-5)
| # | Purpose | Layout | Notes |
|---|---------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Title | title | Impactful, minimal. Product name + one-line tagline |
| 2 | Agenda | content | 3 sections max. Sets expectations |
| 3 | The Problem | image/quote | Emotional hook. Make audience feel the pain |
| 4 | Current State | data | Visualization showing scale of problem |
| 5 | The Opportunity | data | Market size, trend, or gap |

### Act 2: The Solution (slides 6-12)
| # | Purpose | Layout | Notes |
|---|---------|--------|-------|
| 6 | Our Solution | title | Product reveal. Hero shot or demo screenshot |
| 7 | How It Works | split | 3-step process, left text / right visual |
| 8 | Key Benefit 1 | image | One benefit, one visual proof |
| 9 | Key Benefit 2 | image | One benefit, one visual proof |
| 10 | Key Benefit 3 | image | One benefit, one visual proof |
| 11 | Demo/Product | image | Screenshot, video placeholder, or live demo note |
| 12 | Transition | quote | Bridge to proof section |

### Act 3: The Proof (slides 13-18)
| # | Purpose | Layout | Notes |
|---|---------|--------|-------|
| 13 | Case Study 1 | split | Logo + quote + metric |
| 14 | Case Study 2 | split | Logo + quote + metric |
| 15 | Competitive Landscape | data | 2x2 matrix or comparison table |
| 16 | Business Model | content | Revenue streams, pricing |
| 17 | Traction | data | Growth curve, key metrics |
| 18 | Transition | quote | Bridge to future section |

### Act 4: The Path Forward (slides 19-24)
| # | Purpose | Layout | Notes |
|---|---------|--------|-------|
| 19 | Roadmap | content | 3 phases: now, next, future |
| 20 | Team | split | 3-4 key members with one-line credentials |
| 21 | The Ask | title | Investment, partnership, or decision requested |
| 22 | Summary | content | 3 key messages restated |
| 23 | Closing | title | Memorable final thought, callback to opening |
| 24 | Contact | content | How to follow up |

## Visual Design System

### Color Palette
- **Background:** dark gray (#1A1A1A) or deep navy (#0A1628)
- **Primary text:** white (#FFFFFF)
- **Accent 1:** brand color or vibrant blue (#007AFF)
- **Accent 2:** complementary warm tone for data highlights
- **Muted text:** medium gray (#8E8E93) for captions and sources

### Typography
- **Display:** SF Pro Display, Helvetica Neue, or similar sans-serif
- **Body:** SF Pro Text or system sans-serif
- **Headlines:** 44pt Bold, ALL CAPS for impact slides
- **Body:** 20pt Regular
- **Data callouts:** 64pt Bold for hero numbers

### Imagery
- High-resolution photography, dark and moody when on dark backgrounds
- Abstract gradients for transition slides
- Product screenshots on device mockups
- No clip art, no stock photos with watermarks

### Data Visualization
- Minimal chart chrome — no gridlines, no borders
- Single accent color for the key data series
- Gray for comparison/context data
- Always label the insight, not just the axis

## Presenter Guidelines

### Pacing (~20 minute talk)
| Section | Slides | Time |
|---------|--------|------|
| Opening + Problem | 1-5 | 4 min |
| Solution | 6-12 | 6 min |
| Proof | 13-18 | 5 min |
| Path Forward + Close | 19-24 | 5 min |

### Transitions
- Pause 2-3 seconds between sections
- Use a transition slide (quote or bold statement) to signal section shifts
- Never read slides aloud — speak to the audience, not the screen

### Audience Interaction
- After slide 5 (Problem): "Does this resonate with what you're seeing?"
- After slide 12 (Solution): pause for questions before proof section
- Before slide 21 (The Ask): "Before I share what we're looking for..."

## Backup Slides (appendix)
Prepare 5 deep-dive slides for Q&A:
1. Detailed financial projections
2. Technical architecture
3. Full competitive analysis
4. Customer acquisition strategy
5. Risk mitigation plan
